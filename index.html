The problem

Why "reading the Gita" almost never sticks

You don't need more quotes. You don't need an 18-chapter translation sitting on the shelf. You need a path — and one verse a day to walk it. Here's what gets in the way.

📜

Translations read like homework

Most Gita editions were written for scholars. By verse five you've stopped reading — not because the wisdom is hard, but because the language is.

🌊

Quote apps go shallow, fast

A new aesthetic verse every day. No context. No depth. You swipe, you nod, you forget. Six months in, nothing has actually changed.

🧭

There's no sense of where you are

The Gita is a journey, not a feed. Without a path and milestones, every day feels like starting over — and you can't see whether you're getting anywhere.

🤫

No room to sit with the verse

Reading a shloka is one thing. Writing what it asks of you today is where it actually lands. Almost nothing makes this easy or private.

About Wisdom

Built for the Gita as it was meant to be lived.

Not a feed. Not a study guide. A daily walk down one of three classical paths — on the iPhone you already carry.

🪔

What is Wisdom?

Wisdom is the daily Bhagavad Gita app built around three paths — Bhakti, Karma and Jnana. Choose one and you'll receive a single Sanskrit shloka each morning, drawn from that path, with plain meaning, a deeper note, a journal prompt, lock-screen widgets and reminders.

🧘

Who is Wisdom for?

For people who've tried to "read the Gita" and quietly given up, and for long-time practitioners who want a daily ritual that actually moves them through the text. Beginners start on any path; advanced readers can walk all three.

🧭

Why the three-paths design?

Because the Gita itself is built that way. Krishna doesn't teach one method — he teaches three, depending on the seeker. Wisdom mirrors that. Your path determines your daily verse, your milestones, and the shape of the practice you're building.

Wisdom & the three paths.

Wisdom organises the Bhagavad Gita around the three classical paths the Gita itself teaches — Bhakti (devotion), Karma (action) and Jnana (wisdom). You choose one to begin with; your daily verses, milestones and journal prompts are drawn from that path. You can switch paths or explore another any time.
Karma (action) is for those who want to act with steadiness and detachment from outcomes — it suits the doer who's stuck on results. Bhakti (devotion) is for those who want to give themselves fully — to a person, a calling, or the divine. Jnana (wisdom) is for those who want to see clearly through identity and reaction. Most people start with the one that matches where their life currently feels stuck.
